"The Longest of Rope", 23', ©2026 / (no.5,85-26) Micah Weber

Using the filming of an action movie in Braddock, Pennsylvania, as a refrain, «The Longest of Rope» presupposes the camera's gaze to be a pessimistic narrator of its own expenditure. The border between inside and outside, sound and image, is haunted by its inability to cohere into a narrative object. By leaning into a mess of icons, the film obfuscates documentary conventions by deploying classical motifs of pursuit and montage, presenting a perspective that is distanced, fragmented, and deliberately guarded—one alienated from its subject matter: the infrastructures of collapse in the United States, the disguising of a lost cause, and the 2025 protests across Serbia. The result is a film dedicated to the runaway nihilism of an always-already exhausted project of representation.
