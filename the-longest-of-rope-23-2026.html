"The Longest of Rope (w/ Accompaniment)", 23', ©2026 / (no.5,85-26) Micah Weber

Composed around the filming of an action movie in Braddock, Pennsylvania—read here as a refrain—"The Longest of Rope" casts the camera’s gaze as a pessimistic narrator of its own experience. The border between inside and outside, sound and image, is haunted by its inability to cohere into a narrative object. By sorting through a mess of icons—from the American West to an aging author’s need for solitude—the film abstains from documentary conventions. Instead, it plies classical motifs of pursuit and montage to present a perspective that is distanced, fragmented, and deliberately guarded; one alienated from its subject matter, which is treated here as mere background: the 2025 protests across Serbia; the demolition of a small town; and the destruction of another. The result is an anti-narrative documentary of nihilist flight from an always-already exhausted vocabulary of representation.
